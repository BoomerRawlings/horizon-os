Your laptop setup

Your existing Obsidian vault is the source of truth. Horizon connects to that same synced folder and works with the files in place.

There is no import. Horizon does not create a second vault, copy your notes, or merge folders.

1. Finish Obsidian Sync first

  1. Install Obsidian and sign in to Obsidian Sync.
  2. Choose your existing remote vault and let it download completely.
  3. Open the vault in Obsidian and confirm your notes are present.

Do this before selecting the folder in Horizon so it can validate the complete vault structure.

2. Select that vault in Horizon

On first launch, choose the top-level vault folder - the folder containing 00_Index.md, Calendar, Inbox, and Runs.

Horizon remembers this location privately on the laptop and verifies it every time the app starts.

3. Connect integrations

Open Settings > Integrations and connect the services you use. Sign-ins and tokens are intentionally machine-specific and are never synced through Obsidian or committed to GitHub.

  • Obsidian data is already connected through the selected vault.
  • Calendar items, projects, research notes, captures, and vault-backed Horizon settings arrive through Obsidian Sync.
  • Microsoft, Google Drive, Zotero, Codex, and other services need a one-time connection on this laptop.

Changing vaults later

Use Settings > Data & Storage > Change vault. Horizon validates the new folder, saves the pointer locally, and restarts against that exact vault.

Daily workflow

  1. Capture rough tasks, ideas, links, and papers from Home.
  2. Sweep the queue so each item reaches Calendar, Projects, Research, or Notes.
  3. Choose the next priority and use Focus for a bounded work session.

The full guide lives in Settings > Advanced > How to use Horizon.
