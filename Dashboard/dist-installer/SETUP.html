Welcome to Horizon

Everything in orbit

Horizon is your one place to work from — notes, tasks, calendar, projects, files, and apps in a single home base. This page walks you through setup. It takes about two minutes, and you only do it once.

The installer already put Horizon on your PC and created a HorizonOS shortcut on your Desktop and Start menu. If Horizon didn't open on its own, launch it from there.

1 The big idea

Under the hood, Horizon keeps a vault: a plain folder of Markdown files that is the durable source of truth. Everything you do in the app is saved there as readable files you own forever. Horizon is local-first — your data lives on your machine, not in someone else's cloud.

Practical upshot: work inside Horizon, and it quietly keeps a clean, portable archive for you.

2 Point Obsidian at your vault Optional

Horizon reads and writes your vault by itself — you can use it with nothing else installed. Adding the free Obsidian app just gives you a second way to browse and edit the same notes.

  1. Download Obsidian from obsidian.md and install it.
  2. In Obsidian, choose Open folder as vault.
  3. Point it at your Horizon folder (the installer shows the exact path when it finishes; it's usually %USERPROFILE%\HorizonOS).

Edits in Obsidian and Horizon are the same files — always in sync, no import/export.

3 Connect the apps you use Optional

Open Settings → Integrations in Horizon and turn on what you want:

  • Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, OneNote) — opens your locally installed apps. Nothing to configure.
  • Google (Drive, Docs, Sheets, Gmail, Calendar) — opens on the web; optionally connect Drive to browse files inside Horizon.
  • Research — library and scholar search, plus your in-vault research notes.
  • Codex — the assistant that sorts your captures (next step).

Skip anything you don't use. You can add it later at any time.

4 Your daily rhythm

  1. Capture anything — type a thought, task, or link into the Capture bar on Home. It's saved instantly.
  2. Sweep the queue — open the Capture Queue and let Horizon turn each item into a calendar event, note, project, or reference. One click each, fully undoable.
  3. Work from Home — check Today for what's next, spotlight your active project, and run a Focus session for deep work.

5 Where to get help

The full, feature-by-feature manual lives inside the app under Settings → Advanced → How to use Horizon. You can replay the first-run walkthrough from there too.

Updates: Horizon keeps itself current automatically in the background when a new version is available.
